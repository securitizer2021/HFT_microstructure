Data Overview

Each trading day includes millions of tick-level records from CME Treasury futures LOB - limit order books e.g., 2Y, 5Y, 10Y, 30Y, capturing the market‚Äôs microstructure at millisecond granularity across both top-of-book and deeper liquidity levels.

To mitigate overfitting and reduce noise, the data is downsampled‚Äîpreserving key dynamics while filtering out excess volatility. A representative subset is then sampled to balance model fidelity with computational efficiency, producing a high-quality dataset optimized for training short-term market behavior.

Feature Summary

We leverage a curated blend of raw and engineered features derived from high-frequency Treasury futures data. These features capture the real-time behavior of the limit order book (LOB), including price levels, liquidity depth, and trade flow signals. Additional derived metrics‚Äîsuch as microprice, order imbalance, and realized volatility‚Äîenhance the signal quality and reflect nuanced market dynamics.

To streamline modeling and reduce overfitting risk, we apply dimensionality reduction techniques (e.g., PCA), transforming the full feature space into a compact set of informative components. This process ensures robust, efficient learning while preserving key aspects of market microstructure.

Model Training Overview

Our forecasting engine is powered by a deep LSTM architecture with dropout regularization, trained on high-frequency market data. It supports two complementary modes: classification for directional prediction (up, flat, down) and regression for estimating price deltas in basis points (bp).

Temporal sequences span hundreds of events to capture evolving order flow and liquidity dynamics. Prediction horizons are tailored to anticipate forward moves before market impact fully materializes.

Model tuning is guided by Bayesian optimization, which automatically selects the optimal combination of network size, memory depth, dropout, batch size, and learning rate. This achieves the best trade-off between fast reactivity and robust generalization under noisy conditions.

For classification, training labels are derived from price movements exceeding a configurable threshold (e.g., ¬±0.75 bp), enabling the model to detect subtle but meaningful shifts. In regression mode, the model directly learns to forecast mid-price changes in bp.
